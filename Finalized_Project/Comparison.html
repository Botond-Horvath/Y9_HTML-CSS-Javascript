The Lack of Education Around the World

The lack of education is a huge problem around the world, and in fact, in Sub-Saharan Africa, almost 40% of children will never attend secondary school. This is a huge problem, and to add to the problem, almost 14% of the worlds population is illiterate, meaning that they are unable to read and write. For example, in countries like Niger, only 19% of the population is considered to be able to read and write. This is a huge problem in global society, since as UNICEF states:

"every child has the right to learn"

This means that every child should have the right to reach their full potential without running into barriers that others would not.

So What Does This Mean?

Overall, it can be seen that the issues of poverty and the lack of education in the world are closely related, but why? Well, there are many different reasons that can be mentioned, and the interdependace of the two issues can almost be called a societal covalent bond. This is because poverty can obviously lead to the lack of education, and in the same way, the lack of education can lead back to poverty.

So why does poverty lead to the lack of education. Well this might be the obvious answer out of the two sides of this same coin that we are looking at, since if a family lives in extreme poverty, it will not have the adequate finantial position in many cases to be able to pay for education. Even if they are able to pay for primary education for their children, there is a very low change that they will be able to afford secondary education, and virtually no chance for tetrary education. Furthermore, even if we hypothesize that schooling was completely free, in an impoverishd family it would still be very hard for the student to reach their full potential and bring the best out of themselves due to the fact that they would not even be able to attend school, or do their homework. This is because of the lack of time that they have, due to the constat human work that they have to complete, like working on the farm, walking kilometers for drinkable water, or even having to work full time in order to secure a small income for the family. Furthermore, it is much more difficult to study or focus with an empty stomach, or without electricity, internet, or sufficient resources. Therefore, if there is achild in poverty, it is an increadible challange to break out of that cycle.

The second point is that the lack of education, that is as we saw an effect of poverty, is also a cause of it, closing this visious cycle. This is true because if one is less educated, they have less of a chance to be able to secure for themselves a well paying job, or to be able to continue their studies in order to become a highly ranked individual. For example, it is an impossibility for someone to become a doctor or a scientist if they do not even know how to read or write. Also, in order to be considered a scientist or a doctor, one would have to complete a university level education, which is an impossibility if the individual is already struggling with primary education. Furthermore, the lack of education creates teachers for example that are less suited to teaching, or whose knowledge is not satisfactory due to their own lack of being educated. This leads to students not having teachers, which is another lack of resource that is caused by this visious cycle.

Now, we have come to the conclusion that it is virtually impossible to break out of this depressing cycle without some sort of help from the outside world, or governements, and most importantly, you! You have to power to make a change with this problem, since you have the education, and resources presumable that you are reading this.

About

Poverty is a great problem in the world, and is in fact the cause of many other problems that arise in the world society. According to the United Nations, the amount of people living in extreme poverty has been on a steady decline since 1990, but there are still about 767 million people, or about 10% of the world’s population that lives in extreme poverty daily. This number is higher in rural areas: more than 17%, which is more that three times higher than the poverty rate in urban communities. This means that these people are struggling to fulfill their and their families basic needs of health and sanitation, education, or access to water and food. The majority of these people live on less than $1.90 a day, and the majority of them are in sub-saharan Africa. We have made progress in stopping poverty since 1990, but there is another looming risk in our era.

“the pace of change is decelerating and the COVID-19 crisis risks reversing decades of progress in the fight against poverty” - UN

In fact, the UNU World Institute for Development Economics Research suggests that due to the economic fallout caused by the global pandemic, extreme poverty could grow by another half a million people, which would be another 8% of the global population added to the poverty line. This would be the first time that overall poverty would increase in the world since 1990. In fact, the effects of this pandemic are already seen. According to UNICEF, 150 million children have already plunged into poverty due to COVID-19, and that is only data from September. There have since then been two more wavers of COVID-19 that attacked most of the countries in the world, with now variants soaring.

UNICEF states that one in five children in the world experience extreme poverty on a daily basis, and the experience for children can be much more destructive, and much different from adults. The experience of poverty for children can last lifelong. Many basic human rights are taken away from children experiencing poverty, and when they are deprived of these basic needs, this “[diminishes] the life chances ... and their ability to realize their full potential” (UNICEF).

Causes

According to Human Rights Careers, there are many different causes that lead to poverty. The most important cause is generational. Most children in poverty are there because they are born into it, and once they are there, it is very hard to break out of this cycle if the child is not provided with booster support, like scholarships or access to education. This though is a phenomenon, and not a cause, so here are some of the points of why the parents or ancestors of those children were usually there in the first place:

Lack of good jobs or job growth

If you are in an underdeveloped or developing country, it is very hard a lot of the time to find a job, or at least a good paying job. Therefore, you cannot get a formidable income. Furthermore, many traditional workplaces in many countries are disappearing, like farming, and people are finding it hard to adjust.

Lack of quality education

This is probably one of the greatest causes of poverty, since it provides a break in the generational cycle, where new generations can rise above their parents and get better jobs, or even provide better jobs for the less fortunate. This unfortunately is a right that is deprived from many people around the world.

Warfare

When there is a war, people are completely deprived of their everyday lives, and this causes a lot of people to lose their homes, possibly their fathers or husbands, and very importantly their jobs or education. Many getting the worst of it could even be forced to flee.

Climate Change

This is also another man made cause like warfare that has a huge impact on many that usually don’t even cause it. According to the World Bank, climate change has the power to impoverish 100 million people in the next decade. Floods, severe storms, and droughts to name a few have a detrimental effect on village communities or agricultural areas, knocking down the balance that had been here for thousands of years.

Social Injustice

This is a topic that has been around for many thousands of years, and is still to this day relevant in affecting poverty even in developed countries. Many people that belong to a certain race, like black people to name one in the US, are usually not given equal job or educational opportunities as opposed to their white counterparts.

Lack of support

Finally, the lack of support is a problem for people trying to break the cycle of poverty. For example, lack of government funding, infrastructure (like access to water fountains), or healthcare are all barriers to development. Sometimes this is impossible for a government of a developing country to finance, because there is no money, and therefore it is important that us in developed countries also help.

About

Education is a fundamental human right, and not having access to quality education, or education at all, is a huge problem all around the world. Roughly one in five school age children are not in school at all, and 759 million adults around the world are unable to read and write. That is almost 10% of the world population! It is true that there has been some progress, and there are more children and adolescents attending school today than ever before, but according to UNICEF, in the majority of these schools, learning doesn’t happen on the level that it should.

“Schooling does not always lead to learning. Worldwide, there are more non-learners in school than out of school”

Furthermore, UNICEF states that there are many reasons for why young people are unable to attend school, the most prominent barrier being poverty.

“children from the poorest households almost five times more likely to be out of primary school than those from the richest”

Children therefore that are born into a cycle of generational poverty are much less likely to attend or do well in school. This is true even if a school is free, since these families are less likely to think highly of education.

Causes

As said, generational poverty is one of the largest causes of the lack of education, but there are many others.

Disabilities / ethinc minorities

According to UNICEF, these groups of children are more likely to be left behind when it comes to education.

The Ontario Human Rights Association states that children with disabilities find it harder to access edication due to the lack of infrastructure. Furthermore, belonging to an ethnic minority in a country remains a significant barrier to education. According to the Brookings Insitute, many students, including in the US, "routinely receive dramatically different learning opportunities based on their social status". Also, there remains, espetially in the US, a significant gap in funding of less finantially strong areas.

Gender

UNICEF states that only 66 percent of countries have achieved gender parity in primary education. This mostly affects girls negatively, but there are some parts of the world where boys are also negatively affected by this.

Location

UNICEF also states that children from rural areas are more than twice as likely to be out of primary school than their urban peers. It also cites conflict as a large scale barrier to education.

The Global Citizen also states that poor education can turn generational when the lack of well educated teachers becomes a further problem, which it is in many areas.
